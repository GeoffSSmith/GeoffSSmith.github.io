Need a Handicap?

You can obtain a handicap by  submitting 3 cards from rounds played at Wycombe Heights (54 holes in total). You can do this by playing in any of the qualifying competitions, in one of the roll ups, with a Committee member or with any Senior acknowledged as 'trusted' by the Handicap Secretary. If you choose to play in a qualifier or a roll up please let the organiser know you are ‘playing for handicap’ so that they can put you out at the back of field so as not to hold up normal play. If not playing in an organised competition, you should let the Handicap Secretary know, before you play, that your scorecard will be submitted for handicap. You must indicate which tee was used i.e. Yellow or White.   

For handicap allocation purposes, the maximum score that can be recorded for any hole is ‘Double  Par’ (i.e. 6 on a Par 3, 8 on a Par 4 and 10 on a Par 5). So, if during your round, on any hole,  your stroke score is more than a Double Par then please pick up and score your card for that hole with a double par . You do not have to ‘putt out’ on every hole if your score exceeds double par. Please do not hold up players behind you by taking a ten on a par three. Leave your card, signed by yourself and your marker in the Seniors box (just outside the Changing  Rooms).   Please let the Handicap Secretary know you have done so. 
 
The 3 cards are entered into the HandicapMaster computer system and an appropriate handicap is  automatically calculated and ratified by the handicap sub committee. You will be notified of your handicap, which can be up to 54, by email. You will also be able to see your handicap details and details of future qualifying rounds you play via the HandicapMaster website, which can be accessed 
via the Seniors website. It will also be shown on the board in the Mens' Changing Room.
