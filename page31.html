Mike Hands – My Year as Senior’s Captain
At the outset of 2016 my year as senior’s captain appeared to stretch away endlessly into
the future but 12 months later it seemed to have flown by, so much so that in thinking back
now I struggle to remember what actually took place! However, I can say for sure that it
was an enjoyable and very busy year. After handing over the baton to Peter Thorne, I did
say half jokingly that it was like retiring all over again - and my wife agreed as she presented
me with the list of neglected DIY.
As captain there always seemed to be something to sort out after playing golf, mostly
organising matches and other events as well as dealing with the occasional issues, although
very few of these as our senior members are generally a well behaved bunch. One minor
example was the time when the winner of a raffle prize complained that the donated bottle
of wine had already been opened and was half empty (or half full)! We found him another
prize! An interesting challenge at the start of the year was a dispute with one of our Winter
League opponents which ended up being referred to the R&A, so I got to know certain
sections of the Rules of Golf in great detail.
Mostly the year went very smoothly, in no small measure down to the systems and
procedures that had been put in place by previous captains and committees and to the
support of the 2016 committee who as ever were diligent and constructive. It is also
extremely useful to experience the 2 year lead in as a committee member before graduating
to captain. In my case I had organised the Wednesday roll up and the Eligibility and Victor
Ludorum lists in my 1 st year and taken on Vice Captain duties in the 2nd year so I knew what
to expect by the time I took up the reins.
During the year there was some debate in committee about the organisation of away days
and it is very pleasing to see that having been brought fully “in-house” they have gone from
strength to strength and are hugely popular. I had some difficulties during the year in
finding enough seniors to play in all the club matches and as a result we decided to reduce
the number of summer friendly matches from 22 to 16 (now back to 17). Ironically this is
another symptom of the success of a thriving section as there are so many opportunities to
play competitive golf.
Talking of competitions we enjoyed a reasonably successful year in matches against other
clubs and against the ladies section. As is often the case we won all our home matches but
struggled on unfamiliar away courses.
My chosen charity in 2016 was Wycombe Homeless Connection for whom, thanks to the
generosity of members, we raised nearly £4,500.
So, overall I look back fondly on a great year and a great experience which I recommend
wholeheartedly to anyone who isn’t already able to claim his place on the past captain’s
table.
